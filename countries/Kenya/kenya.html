Wildlife and Safari Tourism

Kenya has some of the most diverse and abundant wildlife populations in Africa, making it a top destination for safari tourism

Home of the "Big Five"
TThe term "Big Five" refers to the lion, elephant, buffalo, leopard, and rhinoceros. These animals were considered the most challenging to hunt, and today they’re the stars of Kenya's safari tourism.

The Great Migration
Every year, millions of wildebeest, zebras, and gazelles journey from Tanzania's Serengeti to Kenya's Maasai Mara in search of food and water.

Agro-Tourism

Agro-tourism is an emerging attraction in Kenya, offering visitors a unique opportunity to explore the country’s agricultural heritage and learning about sustainable farming practices.

Tea Plantations in Kericho
The town of Kericho, in Kenya's highlands, is known for its vast tea plantations. Visitors can tour these lush, green plantations, learn about the tea-growing process, and witness the intricate harvesting techniques used by skilled tea pickers.

Coffee Farms in Central Kenya
Central Kenya, particularly the Kiambu and Nyeri regions, is famous for coffee farming. Visitors can tour coffee farms to see how coffee beans are grown, harvested, and processed. These tours often include tasting sessions where visitors can enjoy high-quality Kenyan coffee and learn about the different flavors and techniques that make Kenyan coffee distinct.

Flower Farms near Lake Naivasha
Kenya is a leading exporter of cut flowers, especially roses, which are grown on large flower farms around Lake Naivasha. Visitors can tour these farms to see how flowers are cultivated, harvested, and prepared for export, primarily to Europe.

Authentic Cuisines

Kenyan cuisine is a delightful fusion of African, Indian, and Arab influences. With its bold flavors, aromatic spices, and fresh ingredients, Kenyan food is a celebration of the country's rich heritage and natural abundance.


Nyama Choma
Grilled meat, usually goat or beef, that is marinated and roasted over open coals. It’s commonly enjoyed with kachumbari (a fresh tomato and onion salad) and ugali.

Samosas
Indian-inspired snack, samosas in Kenya are filled with spiced minced meat or vegetables and deep-fried until crispy.

Githeri
hearty dish made from boiled maize and beans, often mixed with vegetables, potatoes, and sometimes meat. It’s a common comfort food in Kenya.
